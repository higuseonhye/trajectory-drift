# Organizational trajectory drift

Operational patterns when **teams and systems** lose coherence — not only when models hallucinate.

---

## Mission drift

**Symptoms:** Execution continues; strategic intent quietly shifts. OKRs and daily work diverge.

**Causes:** Weak reinforcement of mission in handoffs; local optimization; missing propagation of “why.”

**Recovery:** Mission checkpoint at delegation boundaries; explicit non-goals in org memory.

---

## Execution fragmentation

**Symptoms:** Many parallel initiatives; few closed loops; high activity, low propagation.

**Causes:** Interaction starvation between teams; unclear ownership; entropy from urgent overrides.

**Recovery:** Loop closure metrics; reduce WIP at coordination layer.

---

## Organizational entropy

**Symptoms:** Policies exist but are not enforced in workflows; incidents repeat; culture of “ship anyway.”

**Causes:** Stale org memory; no field-level validation of constraints across agents and humans.

**Recovery:** Org memory layer tied to live runs; incident-aware guards (see demo org memory).

---

## Relationship drift

**Symptoms:** Key partnerships or internal alliances stop producing momentum; coordination fatigue rises.

**Causes:** Async gaps; unresolved override conflicts; social reinforcement gaps.

**Recovery:** Human–AI coherence panel patterns applied to team interaction maps.

---

## Culture degradation

**Symptoms:** Tone preserved; standards erode; “confidently wrong” becomes normalized.

**Causes:** Recursive delegation without grounding; reward for velocity over continuity.

**Recovery:** Grounding pins; calibration journals at org level; coordination failure archive reviews.
